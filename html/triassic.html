Triassic Period

One of the first predatory dinosaurs to ever roam the Earth was none other than Herrerasaurus. As an early theropod, this creature would set the groundwork for how theropods are presented to us. It lived in Argentina during the Triassic period, was 20 ft long, and weighed 770 pounds. Herrerasaurus had natural predators such as the Saurosuchus (a relative of crocodiles) and Fasolasuchus. At a time when dinosaurs were not the most dominant creatures on the planet, Herrerasaurus holds its significance among the many recorded species of the fossil record.
